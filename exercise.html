What is a CPU?

The digestive system is the core component that fuels your body, and while it is essential, it can only function optimally with the support of other systems. The stomach and intestines break down food and absorb nutrients, working alongside other organs like the liver, kidneys, and heart. Just as the digestive system is separate from the brain, which controls thought and movement, it is also separate from the muscles, which help you move and perform physical tasks. Each system plays its part to ensure your body functions as a whole.

Just as CPUs are built from billions of tiny transistors that process information, your body relies on countless cells and processes to carry out essential functions. These cells work together, sending signals and performing actions that allow you to move, think, and even digest food. For example, your nervous system transmits electrical impulses to control your muscles, while your digestive enzymes break down food into nutrients. These tiny "switches" help your body function smoothly, supporting everything you do, whether it's exercising or simply enjoying a meal.

Just as a computer's CPU processes instructions through a cycle of fetching, decoding, and executing, our digestive system operates through a series of stages to efficiently process the food we consume.
